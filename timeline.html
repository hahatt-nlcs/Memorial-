Key Events of the Holocaust

Jan 30, 1933
Adolf Hitler is appointed Chancellor of Germany, marking the beginning of Nazi rule.
Sep 15, 1935
The Nuremberg Laws are enacted, stripping Jews of citizenship and rights.
Nov 9–10, 1938
Kristallnacht: A violent pogrom against Jews throughout Nazi Germany and Austria.
Sep 1, 1939
Germany invades Poland; World War II begins. Ghettos and deportations follow.
Jun 22, 1941
Operation Barbarossa begins. Einsatzgruppen initiate mass shootings of Jews in the East.
Jan 20, 1942
Wannsee Conference: Nazi officials plan the "Final Solution" — the systematic extermination of Jews.
Jul 23, 1944
Soviet troops liberate Majdanek, the first major concentration camp to be freed.
Jan 27, 1945
Auschwitz is liberated by the Red Army — now commemorated as International Holocaust Remembrance Day.
May 8, 1945
Victory in Europe Day (V-E Day): Nazi Germany surrenders. The Holocaust ends.
